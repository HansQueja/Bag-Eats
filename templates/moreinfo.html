How It Works?

You are what you eat

CHOOSE

Users enter their total budget for food. The users will select the category of their meals. They can also specify dietary preferences and restrictions, such as avoiding certain allergens or selecting specific food categories.

PROCESS

The system uses the input budget and preferences to filter relevant food items from the database.

The Knapsack Problem algorithm processes the filtered data to determine the optimal combination of food items that maximizes the combined rating (considering price, nutrition, and taste)without exceeding the budget.

OUTPUT

The system recommends a list of food items that offer the best value within the user's budget. These recommendations take into account user preferences like meal type and dietary restrictions.

SEARCH AND FILTER FEATURE

Users can refine their recommendations further using search filters based on specific criteria such as meal time (breakfast, lunch, dinner) or specific nutritional needs.
